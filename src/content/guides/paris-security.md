Paris is a major capital with the ordinary security profile of one: largely safe day to day, with petty crime concentrated in predictable places and times. For diplomatic staff there is also a layer of status-related awareness. This guide covers sensible everyday defaults. Where your mission has a regional security officer or formal security policy, that policy is the authority and overrides anything general written here.

## Where mission policy takes over

Before relying on personal judgement, establish what your mission already mandates.

- Is there a regional security officer or designated security contact? *[Confirm on arrival.]*
- What is the mission's policy on residence security, travel and incident reporting? *[Obtain it in writing.]*
- What are the emergency and duty-officer contacts, and how do you reach them out of hours? *[Confirm and store these before you need them.]*

The single most useful step is knowing exactly who to call and how, before an incident — not during one.

## Everyday personal security

The common risks for residents and visitors in Paris are opportunistic: pickpocketing and bag-snatching in crowded transport hubs, tourist sites and on certain metro lines, and distraction scams. Defaults that lower exposure:

- Keep valuables and documents secured and out of sight in crowds and on transport.
- Be deliberate with phones in stations and at café terraces, common snatch points.
- Carry copies of key documents separately from the originals.
- Vary routines where your role makes that prudent.

## Residential security

- Understand the building's existing security and who holds keys and access.
- Confirm what your mission requires or provides for residences, and what you may or may not alter as a tenant. *[Verify — depends on lease and mission policy.]*
- Know your local emergency numbers and the nearest police station.

## Emergency numbers

France uses the European emergency number **112**, reachable across the EU and from mobiles. Country-specific services (police, medical, fire) also have dedicated numbers.

- Save 112 and the local service numbers your mission specifies. *[Confirm the current list with your mission or official sources.]*

## Travel within and beyond France

- For travel outside the capital or abroad, follow your mission's travel-clearance and notification rules. *[Verify the policy.]*
- Check official travel advice from your foreign ministry for any onward destinations.

## Keeping perspective

The aim is calibrated awareness, not anxiety. Most postings to Paris are uneventful from a security standpoint. The disciplined parts — knowing your contacts, following mission policy, securing documents and valuables — are what make the everyday parts easy. Treat the specifics of policy and contacts as things to confirm with your mission rather than to infer.
