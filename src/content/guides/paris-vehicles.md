## The first question: do you actually need a car?

Paris is one of the best-served capitals in Europe for public transport. The Métro, RER, Transilien and an extensive bus network cover virtually every arrondissement and connect to the Île-de-France suburbs. For most postings within Paris itself, including the diplomatic concentrations of the 7th, 8th and 16th arrondissements, a car is a choice, not a necessity.

The picture changes if your children are enrolled at a school in the western suburbs (Saint-Cloud, Croissy-sur-Seine, Maisons-Laffitte), if your posting involves regular travel across the Île-de-France, or if a vehicle is part of your mission's practical requirements. Many diplomatic families in Paris use a car primarily for weekend and holiday travel, not daily commuting.

Parking in Paris is expensive and logistically demanding. CD plates do not confer automatic parking privileges in the city. Before importing or purchasing a vehicle, assess whether regular use justifies the administrative process that follows.

## The legal framework

Diplomatic vehicle registration in France is governed by the [Arrêté du 9 février 2009 relatif aux modalités d'immatriculation des véhicules](https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr), which defines the categories of diplomatic plates and the procedures for their attribution. The underlying entitlements derive from the [Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (1961)](https://legal.un.org/ilc/texts/instruments/english/conventions/9_1_1961.pdf) and the [Vienna Convention on Consular Relations (1963)](https://legal.un.org/ilc/texts/instruments/english/conventions/9_2_1963.pdf).

The two bodies that jointly manage the process in France are the [Sous-direction des Privilèges et Immunités Diplomatiques et Consulaires](https://www.diplomatie.gouv.fr/fr/le-ministere-et-son-reseau/privileges-et-immunites-diplomatiques-et-consulaires/) (MEAE) and the [Direction Générale des Douanes et Droits Indirects, Franchises Diplomatiques](https://www.douane.gouv.fr) (DGDDI).

## Plate categories

French diplomatic plates are green with the following series:

- **CMD** (Chef de Mission Diplomatique): orange lettering
- **CD** (Corps Diplomatique, diplomatic agents): orange lettering
- **C** (Consulaire, consular officers): white lettering
- **K** (Personnel administratif, technique et de service): white lettering

The series attributable to you depends on your accreditation category as recorded by the MEAE. The code is not self-selected. For permanent missions to international organisations (UNESCO, OECD, etc.), a country prefix precedes a country code incremented by 200.

---

## Eligibility and vehicle limits

Entitlement to a diplomatic plate depends on holding a valid titre de séjour spécial issued by the MEAE:

- Holders of **CMD/D** or **CD/D** titles: one vehicle in the corresponding series. A second vehicle in the CD series may be authorised if the holder is married and the spouse is not a French national.
- Holders of **AT/D** or **K**-type titles: one vehicle in the K series, including for married couples.

A new immatriculation in the same series cannot ordinarily be requested within two years of the previous authorisation date. The two-year period runs from the date the DGDDI issued the franchise authorisation, not from the registration date.

## The registration procedure

All requests pass through your mission. The mission submits documentation to the MEAE (Sous-direction des Privilèges et Immunités), which coordinates with the DGDDI. The DGDDI issues the franchise authorisation; the Agence Nationale des Titres Sécurisés ([ANTS](https://immatriculation.ants.gouv.fr/demarches-en-ligne/realiser-une-demarche-liee-a-un-vehicule-diplomatique)) then issues the certificat d'immatriculation.

**Documents typically required (confirm with your mission):**

- Formulaire 2 (Demande d'immatriculation en série CMD, CD ou K): six originals with mission stamp
- Certificat d'exonération de la TVA et des droits d'accises (Formulaire 1): six originals
- Titre de séjour spécial (carte diplomatique)
- Certificate of conformity for the vehicle
- Original purchase invoice (with translation if not in French)
- Attestation d'assurance in force

**Processing time:** Allow four to five weeks from complete submission to receipt of the certificat d'immatriculation. A Certificat Provisoire d'Immatriculation (CPI) permits circulation in metropolitan France for three months while the permanent card is issued.

## Customs and tax relief

Vehicles registered under diplomatic plates are exempt from VAT and customs duties at the point of purchase or import, on the basis of the franchise accorded by the DGDDI. The vehicle is imported under a regime of *franchise temporaire*; the Formulaire 2 is the importation document for customs purposes.

This exemption carries obligations: the vehicle may not be lent, rented or sold without first regularising the customs and fiscal situation. On any transfer of ownership or end of mission, the holder must present the vehicle to the competent customs office (the bureau de douane to which the mission is attached) to regularise duties and obtain a Certificat 846A, which is required for re-registration in a normal series.

---

## Insurance

All vehicles circulating in France, including those on diplomatic plates, must carry at minimum third-party liability insurance (*responsabilité civile*) under French law. Diplomatic immunity does not exempt a vehicle from the insurance requirement. Several insurers in France offer policies structured for diplomatically-registered vehicles; your mission's administrative office will know which options are commonly used.

---

## End of mission

On cessation of functions, diplomatic plates must be returned to the competent customs authority. The mission coordinates this process. If you wish to keep the vehicle and re-register it in a normal French series, the customs duties and VAT deferred at the time of the franchise become payable. The vehicle then enters the standard registration system via the ANTS portal.

---

## A note on Vigipirate

France has maintained its national security alert at *Urgence attentat* (Attack Emergency), the highest Vigipirate level, since January 2025. This affects vehicle security practice in certain contexts. Consult your mission's security officer for current guidance on parking and use of official vehicles.
