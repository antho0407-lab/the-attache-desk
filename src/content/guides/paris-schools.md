Schooling is often the decision that shapes everything else about a family posting — where you live, how long you stay, and how smoothly the move lands for children. Paris offers an unusually wide range of options, which is a luxury and a complication. This guide sets out the main systems and the practical mechanics. It deliberately names categories rather than ranking specific schools, and any figure or deadline is flagged for you to confirm directly with the school.

## The main routes

- **The French system.** State (*public*) and private (*privé*) schools following the national curriculum, leading to the *baccalauréat*. Strong continuity if you expect a longer stay or want children integrated locally.
- **International sections within French schools.** Bilingual streams that combine the French curriculum with another national programme.
- **International and foreign-system schools.** Schools teaching in English or another language, often following the **International Baccalaureate**, a British, American or another national curriculum. These ease transfer between postings.
- **Your sending state's own school**, where one exists in Paris.

Which route fits depends on the length of posting, your children's ages and current curriculum, language, and the likely next posting.

## Admissions and timing

The single most common mistake is starting late. Sought-after places — particularly in English-medium and IB schools — can be limited and competitive, and the French academic year and admissions cycle may not line up with your home calendar.

- When the relevant school year starts, and the admissions deadlines for your preferred options. *[Confirm directly with each school.]*
- Whether places are available mid-year if your posting starts off-cycle. *[Verify per school.]*
- Documents required (prior school records, often translated; proof of address; proof of status). *[Confirm requirements.]*
- Assessment or language requirements for entry. *[Verify per school.]*

> Begin enquiries as soon as the posting is confirmed, before housing is fixed if necessary — school availability sometimes drives the choice of neighbourhood, not the other way around.

## Fees and support

Fees vary widely by system: state French schooling is broadly low-cost, while international and foreign-system schools can be substantial.

- Fee levels for your shortlist, and what they include. *[Confirm with each school — figures change yearly.]*
- Whether your mission provides an education allowance, and what it covers. *[Verify entitlement with your mission — do not assume.]*

## Continuity between postings

If further moves are likely, weigh curriculum portability. The IB and well-established national systems transfer more predictably across countries than a route that ties children closely to one national curriculum.

## A workable sequence

1. Define constraints: ages, current curriculum, language, likely length of posting.
2. Shortlist by **system**, then by school.
3. Contact schools early for availability, deadlines and requirements.
4. Confirm any mission education allowance.
5. Let school availability inform the housing search (see [Living in Paris](/guides-by-city/paris/living)).

Names, fees and deadlines move every year, so this guide stops at the level of systems and process. Confirm the current specifics with the schools themselves before you commit.
